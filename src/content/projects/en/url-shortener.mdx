## Context

A URL shortener is famously simple to build — and famously hard to build *well* at scale.
I used it as a vehicle to practice the parts of engineering that a monolith hides:
service boundaries, deployment pipelines, caching strategy and observability.

## Problem

The interesting problems only appear when you refuse the easy path: what happens on cache
miss storms? How do you deploy the redirect service without dropping a single request?
How do you know the analytics pipeline silently broke?

## Solution

Three services with strict contracts:

- **Shortener service** — write path, collision-safe ID generation, validation
- **Redirect service** — hot read path, Redis cache-first with PostgreSQL fallback
- **Analytics service** — async click events over a queue, aggregation jobs

The infrastructure is **fully declarative**: Terraform provisions the cluster, Kubernetes
manifests describe the services, and Grafana dashboards + alerts cover the golden signals
(latency, traffic, errors, saturation).

## Outcome

- Redirect p99 latency under 10 ms with warm cache
- Rolling deploys with zero dropped requests
- Every incident debuggable from dashboards and traces alone

## What I learned

Observability is a feature you build *first*, not last — without it you're not running
a system, you're guessing at one.
